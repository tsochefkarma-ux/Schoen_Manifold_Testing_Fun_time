## Part IV - Three-Torsion Observability and the Limits of Residue Transport

**Paper:** `three_torsion_observability.pdf`

Part IV asks whether the exact residue-transport mechanism of Part III can be used in reverse to determine degree-three torsion-resolved series.

Main results:

- the complete degree-two ambiguity has bare transport selectivity zero;
- the degree-three weight-16/index-3 HSS torsion image satisfies one exact all-orders specialization relation;
- the global symmetric ambiguity reduces from 21 coefficients to a 15-dimensional observable quotient;
- the two parked sectors are faithful on that quotient;
- the scalar curvature observable sees only two directions, while the full local response decomposes as 14 exchange-even plus 1 exchange-odd direction;
- nine torsion-summed coefficients plus six coefficients from the mixed sector `(omega,omega2)` reconstruct all 15 observable coefficients through an exact invertible matrix.

The paper does not yet claim a newly derived mixed-sector series. The next stage is the BKOS-to-HSS torsion and multicover dictionary.
